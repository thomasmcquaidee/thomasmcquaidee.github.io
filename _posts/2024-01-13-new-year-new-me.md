For me, 2023 was a pretty cool year. 

I had my graduation for my HND, and I completed my degree in Music Technology. I'm incredibly proud of the work I did throughout my HND, I can probably dedicate an entire post to the two years I spent at college and how much I enjoyed it, and although I didn't have as good of a time at university for the top-up year, I'm still proud of the work I did and for getting through it and leaving with a 2:1.

I also properly began my career in the music industry! Whilst I've *technically* been a earning money from music since 2020, this was the first time where sound work went from a side job to my only source of income. I got to work my first festival stage, the Caffè Nero tent at the Black Deer Festival of Americana, I've assisted on countless mixes for some incredible tracks at the Dolby Atmos studio I work at, and my freelance work has slowly been taking off. It still feels a bit ridiculous that I'm actually working as an audio engineer, this is something I've been working towards since I was at least 15, so I'm extremely grateful to be doing this.

I also got cats! This was a bigger deal for me than I think I realised, I'd always wanted pets growing up, but all I managed to convince my parents to get me was a goldfish, so when I had the realisation that I had steady income, my girlfriend also wanted cats, and we were allowed them at the flat, we ended up finding the best two guys in the world, Mochi and Kiwi!

I've been thinking a lot about what I want to get out of 2024. I've never been great at new year's resolutions, so instead I've come up with some general goals that I want to work towards.

I want sustainability in my career. The music industry is tough, and I'm so incredibly lucky that I've managed to get my foot in the door. Now I want to figure out how to stay there for the rest of my working life without burning out.

I want to spend more time with friends. Leaving college and university has made me realise how much I'd rely on proximity within my friendships, and I've realised quite quickly that I'd need to make much more of an effort to see people now that we're all going our separate ways. I felt last year that I didn't make enough time for my friends, and that's something that I want to work on this year.

I also want a better relationship with social media. I find myself doom-scrolling too much, made even worst when reddit's price increases led to Apollo, my client of choice, shutting down. Whilst I don't think the amount of time I spent on reddit was great, at least I could curate it more to my tastes, and now that I don't have access to that I've found myself replacing it with lower quality content on Instagram reels. I've tried alternatives, like Bluesky and Mastodon, or curation with RSS feeds, but nothing has quite stuck, so I want to work on having a more mindful approach to the internet and social media this year.
